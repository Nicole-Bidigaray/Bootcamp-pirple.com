Recipe of Mashed Potatoes

 Mashed potato (British English) or mashed potatoes (American English and Canadian English), colloquially known as mash, is a dish prepared by mashing boiled, peeled potatoes. Milk and butter are frequently used in preparation and it is frequently whipped at the end. The dish is usually a side dish to meat and/or vegetables. The closely related smashed potatoes dish is made with unskinned potatoes and it is hand mashed and not whipped.

Advantages and culinary preferences:

 Mashed potatoes is a affordable and tasty side dish available for any self-respeting family that sticks to regular healthy and well-fed food with quick, nice and simple preparation capacity. Mash is traditional universal appropriatable dish that gives a lot of positive and completable tasteful affect to consumers of this food.

3 portions

Ingredients:

  • cleaned up potatoes
      • — 10 units
  • salt
      • — 1 tablespoon
  • butter
      • — 1 ounce
  • water
      • — 0,6 gallon

Preparation:

  1. Clean up potatoes, cut every unit to eight parts and wash it well. 🍠
  2. Pour out potatoes in stock pot with water and heat it to a boil. ♨
  3. After boil pour out salt in water and continue heat it on half of firepower twenty minutes. 🔥
  4. Once heat is over pour out some little water to a dish and almost all water from a stock pot. 🥣
  5. Pour out butter in stock pot and mash potatoes very well, add some water from dish for making food least dry. 🍲
  6. Pour out food carefuly to your dish and apply it to the dining table with taste. 🍽
  7. Consume it nicely with taste and other food on dinner table. 🛎

Dish representation

 Represented dish may have different image and taste depending from your cooking purposes, ingredient composing and sorts of potatoes. Usually it has well saturated yellow warm color, warm smell and easy well taste and density.
 Mashed potato can be served as a side dish, and is often served with sausages in the British Isles, where they are known as bangers and mash. Mashed potato can be an ingredient of various other dishes, including shepherd's and cottage pie, pirogi, colcannon, dumplings, potato pancakes, potato croquettes and gnocchi. Particularly runny mashed potatoes are called mousseline potatoes.
 In the United Kingdom, the cold mashed potato is mixed with fresh eggs and then fried until crisp to produce the potato cake. This dish is thought to have originated in Cornwall and is a popular breakfast item. When instead combined with meat and other leftover vegetables, the fried dish is known as bubble and squeak.
 A popular accompaniment to mashed potatoes in the United States is gravy. The most common forms of gravy paired with mashed potatoes are beef gravy or turkey gravy, though vegetable gravy is becoming more common as the vegetarian and vegan trends see a rise in popularity.
